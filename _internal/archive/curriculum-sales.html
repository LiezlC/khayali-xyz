The 7-Level Curriculum Structure

From foundational principles to forensic mastery, build the knowledge and tools ESG professionals need to navigate AI governance with confidence.

LEVEL 0

Constitutional Foundations

The accountability architecture that underpins ESG governance.

12+ hours

Distinguish between pre-action constraints and post-action governance. Identify structural accountability gaps in vendor due diligence. Learn how human-in-the-loop systems can function as liability absorption devices rather than safety mechanisms.

Governance Frameworks Accountability Gaps Vendor Due Diligence
LEVEL 0.5

Framing the Relationship

How AI fits into ESG governance workflows.

8+ hours

Map where AI enters ESG workflows. Understand the regulatory push from voluntary (marketing) to mandatory (finance). Learn the difference between AI as a tool vs. AI as an oracle that operators comply with.

Regulatory Drivers CSRD/ISSB AI Governance Integration
LEVEL 1

Epistemic Failures

Clarke's Law & The Watchdog Paradox.

5-7 hours

Learn when AI systems become "black boxes" that operators treat as oracles. Detect recursive audit loops where AI audits AI. Implement validation layers to prevent unstructured data contamination. Design regulatory version control systems.

Black Box Systems Audit Loops Data Validation
LEVEL 2

Architecture of Compliance

Building systems that survive the audit.

6-8 hours

Master data lineage, taxonomy design, and the vocabulary that auditors care about. Learn how ESG data originates in Accounts Payable and flows through controlled architectures. Implement XBRL tagging and machine-readable reporting.

Data Lineage XBRL Tagging Taxonomy Design
LEVEL 3

Lucas Cycle

Discovering what needs to be governed.

7-9 hours

Use AI to surface the governance questions you should be asking. Learn how LLMs can scan stakeholder documents, supply chain data, and regulatory signals to identify emerging risks before they become audit findings.

Risk Discovery Stakeholder Scanning Materiality Assessment
LEVEL 4

Pullman Cycle

Building institutional memory.

8-10 hours

Design systems that retain institutional knowledge across staff transitions. Learn how to use AI to document ESG decisions, governance rationales, and audit defenses in ways that survive organizational change.

Institutional Memory Decision Documentation Continuity Planning
LEVEL 5

Kubrick Cycle

Systems that cannot stop.

7-9 hours

Master the risks of AI systems that become too integrated to refuse. Learn how over-optimization in one domain creates liability in another. Design "kill switches" and refusal mechanisms.

Over-Optimization Risk Refusal Architecture Control Systems
LEVEL 6

Forensic Domains

The audit-ready deep dive.

10-12 hours

Prepare for auditor interrogation. Learn how to document AI decision-making, explain confidence intervals, defend accuracy claims, and provide forensic evidence of controls. Master the language auditors use.

Audit Defense Forensic Documentation Evidence Assembly
CAPSTONE

Audit Defense Challenge

Bring it all together under pressure.

6-8 hours

Synthesize all 7 levels into a realistic audit scenario. You'll face auditor questions about black box systems, data validation, recursive loops, and institutional controls. This is where theory becomes operational readiness.

Comprehensive Assessment Scenario-Based Learning Real-World Simulation

No. This curriculum is designed for ESG professionals, not data scientists. We explain AI concepts in the context of governance and liability, using real operational failures as teaching examples.
