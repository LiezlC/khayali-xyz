The Problem of Distance

"A diagram appears showing the Solar System, then zooming out to show nearby stars, then our galaxy, then the local group of galaxies."

Our Solar System
Light crosses it in about 11 hours. Voyager 1 needed 35 years just to reach its edge.
Nearby Stars
Proxima Centauri, our closest neighbour: 4.24 light-years. Our fastest probes would take roughly 70,000 years.
The Milky Way
More than 100,000 light-years across; over 100 billion stars, nearly all forever beyond a rocket's reach.
The Local Group
Dozens of galaxies spread across roughly 10 million light-years.

*No chemical rocket crosses these gulfs within a human life. Hence the dream charted below: don't walk the map — fold it.*
