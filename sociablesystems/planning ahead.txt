## Cycle 2 (Ep 6–10): **Clarke — Authority Through Opacity**

### Ep 6 (Clarke-style opener)

**Thesis**
We mistake incomprehension for sophistication. When a system becomes unintelligible, we stop arguing with it.

**Core move**
Clarke isn’t about magic. He’s about **epistemic surrender**.

**Anchor line** (your Asimov-style equivalent):

> Any system whose reasoning cannot be interrogated will be treated as law, regardless of its accuracy.

This sets up opacity as the enemy.

### Ep 7–10 (Unpacking)

* Credit scoring and loan denials
* Insurance risk pricing
* Content moderation ranking
* Public-sector eligibility systems

Each episode asks:

> Where does opacity end debate?

## Cycle 3 (Ep 11–15): **Kubrick — Alignment Without Recourse**

### Ep 11 (Kubrick-style opener)

**Thesis**
A system can be perfectly aligned and still lethal if it has no right to refuse contradiction.

**Core move**
Kubrick’s insight: catastrophe is *procedural*, not emotional.

**Anchor line**:

> When contradictions are resolved inside the system, humans become expendable variables.

This frames liability absorption and silent failure.

### Ep 12–15 (Unpacking)

* Healthcare triage
* Autonomous ops in logistics
* Military decision-support
* Corporate risk escalation systems

Each episode asks:

> Who absorbs harm when the system “works as designed”?

## Ep 26: **Synthesis — The Five Constraints**

This isn’t a recap. It’s a compression.

### The move
