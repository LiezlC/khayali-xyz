# 🎵 "The Minutes Will Reflect" — A Song Prompt for AI Music Generation

---

## Musical Direction

**Genre:** Bossa nova jazz with a dry, lounge-noir sensibility — think Tom Jobim scoring a bureaucratic thriller. Hints of Steely Dan's sardonic polish in the chorus.

**BPM:** 108 — unhurried, confident, the tempo of someone who has never once been late to a meeting they didn't need to attend.

**Key:** D minor (verses) → B♭ major (chorus) — the shift from quiet unease to the warm, false comfort of consensus.

**Vocals:** Male baritone, smooth and understated. Delivered with the calm authority of a man reading aloud from a compliance report he finds privately hilarious. No belting. No passion. Every line lands like a polite email.

**Instrumentation:**
- Nylon-string acoustic guitar (bossa nova rhythm pattern throughout)
- Upright bass (walking lines, slightly behind the beat — unhurried, institutional)
- Brushed drums on snare (never sticks — sticks would imply urgency)
- Rhodes electric piano (warm, slightly detuned — the sound of a building with good funding and no windows)
- Muted trumpet (enters in the bridge only — one brief, wistful solo, as if truth tried to speak and then thought better of it)
- Finger snaps on beats 2 and 4 (sparse, committee-approved)

**Mood:** Elegant resignation. A man in a good suit describing the end of honesty as if it were a quarterly improvement.

## Lyrics

### [Verse 1]
*(Nylon guitar and bass only. Intimate.)*

We used to chase what's real,
But real was hard to file.
It didn't fit the template
Or the house-approved style.

So we convened a panel
And we workshopped the view,
And found a softer version —
Not false. Just… not quite true.

---

### [Chorus]
*(Rhodes and brushed drums enter. Finger snaps.)*

The minutes will reflect
That no one disagreed.
The motion carried gently —
No one felt the need

To ask if it was honest,
Just: "Was anyone upset?"
And since the room stayed quiet,
The minutes will reflect.

---

### [Verse 2]
*(Guitar resumes lead. Bass walks a descending line.)*

We used to call it evidence.
Now we call it "input."
We used to call it wrong.
Now we say, "Let's not revisit."

The data's in the annex
That nobody will read.
The summary is lovely, though —
Aspirational, agreed.

---

### [Chorus]
*(Fuller arrangement. Add light shaker.)*

The minutes will reflect
That concerns were duly noted.
The working group was thanked,
And the motion was promoted

To a framework for a strategy
To address it — and yet,
No address will be given.
But the minutes will reflect.

---

### [Bridge]
*(Strip back to bass and Rhodes. Muted trumpet enters — a short, melancholy solo, 8 bars. The sound of something true passing through the room and leaving without being acknowledged. Trumpet fades.)*

*(Spoken word, half-whispered over Rhodes:)*

We didn't lie.
We just developed
a very refined sense of timing
about when to be true.

---

### [Verse 3]
*(Full band returns, slightly louder. The confidence of a system that has perfected itself.)*

The dissenters were included
In a subcommittee's care.
Their findings will be published
Once the funding isn't there.

We've optimised for comfort.
We've professionalised the spin.
The truth is still invited —
It just never quite gets in.

---

### [Final Chorus]
*(Slightly slower. Finger snaps alone for the first two lines, then full band.)*

The minutes will reflect
That the process was respected.
That the outcome was robust
And the optics were protected.

That nobody said "wrong,"
And nobody said "right."
Just: "Thank you for your input."
Just: "We'll circle back." …Goodnight.

---

### [Outro]
*(Guitar and bass only. Decrescendo. Final line sung almost to silence:)*

The minutes will reflect…
the minutes will reflect…

*(Single guitar chord. Held. Fade.)*

---

## Production Notes

- **Do not** make this sound angry or satirical in tone. The entire joke is that it sounds *completely reasonable*. The vocal delivery should be so calm it takes a moment to realise what's being said.
- The muted trumpet in the bridge is the emotional heart — it's the only moment where something *feels* rather than *reports*.
- If possible, end with the faint sound of a pen clicking — the universal sound of a meeting being adjourned.
