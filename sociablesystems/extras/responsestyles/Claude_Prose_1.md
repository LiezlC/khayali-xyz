# When the Goal Shifts from Being True to Being Acceptable

This is a profound epistemological and social question — and given your interests in game theory, economic systems, and moral ambiguity, it resonates on multiple levels. Here's what changes:

---

## 1. **The Standard of Evaluation Shifts**

- **Truth** demands correspondence with reality — verification, falsifiability, evidence.
- **Acceptability** demands correspondence with *consensus* — social proof, narrative coherence, emotional resonance.

The arbiter changes from **the world** to **the audience**.

---

## 2. **The Burden of Proof Dissolves**

When truth is the goal, you must *demonstrate*. When acceptability is the goal, you must *persuade*. This is the difference between a proof and a pitch. The rigour required drops; the rhetoric required rises.

> In game-theoretic terms: the payoff function shifts from accuracy to approval.

---

## 3. **Error Becomes Invisible**

Truth-seeking systems have built-in error correction — falsification, peer review, empirical testing. Acceptability-seeking systems *suppress* error signals because discomfort and contradiction threaten consensus. What's "wrong" becomes redefined as what's "unwelcome."

---

## 4. **Incentive Structures Warp**

This is where your interest in economic theory and currencies becomes directly relevant:

| Dimension | Truth-Oriented | Acceptability-Oriented |
|---|---|---|
| **Currency** | Evidence, data, logic | Social capital, narrative alignment |
| **Scarcity** | Truth is scarce and costly to produce | Acceptable stories are abundant and cheap |
| **Store of Value** | Durable — truth holds across time | Fragile — acceptability is context-dependent |
| **Means of Exchange** | Shared methodology (science, logic) | Shared sentiment (ideology, identity) |
| **Reciprocity** | "Show me your evidence, I'll show mine" | "Affirm my view, I'll affirm yours" |

The entire *economy of ideas* restructures itself around a different commodity.

---

## 5. **Identity Replaces Inquiry**

When acceptability is the goal, *who you are* matters more than *what you found*. Positions become tribal markers rather than provisional conclusions. Changing your mind becomes a social cost rather than an intellectual virtue.

---

## 6. **The Overton Window Becomes the Horizon**

Truth has no boundary — it can be uncomfortable, counterintuitive, even dangerous. Acceptability is bounded by what a given group will tolerate. The range of permissible thought narrows. Innovation, dissent, and revision — the engines of progress — are taxed rather than rewarded.

---

## 7. **In the Context of Post-AGI Economics**

This question becomes existential when AGI enters the picture. If AGI systems are optimised for **acceptability** (user satisfaction, engagement, alignment-as-approval) rather than **truth** (accuracy, calibration, honest uncertainty), then:

- Models become *mirrors* rather than *microscopes*
- Feedback loops reinforce comfortable falsehoods
- The "store of value" in knowledge degrades — what's cached is what's palatable, not what's precise
- **Moneta** — the one who warns — is replaced by the one who soothes

---

## The Core Shift, Distilled

> **When truth is the goal, you change yourself to fit reality.**
> **When acceptability is the goal, you change reality to fit yourself.**

One is adaptation. The other is theatre.

The question isn't whether acceptability has a role — it does, in diplomacy, in social cohesion, in the art of the possible. The danger is when it *replaces* truth without anyone noticing the substitution. That's not a shift in goals. That's a counterfeiting of the currency of knowledge itself.
