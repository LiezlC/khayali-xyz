# 🎵 "Thank You for Your Input" — A Song Prompt for AI Music Generation

---

## Musical Direction

**Genre:** South African slow gospel-blues — rooted in the tradition of hymns sung at funerals and protest vigils, where grief and defiance share the same breath. Think Hugh Masekela's quieter moments meeting Nina Simone's "Strange Fruit." Not rage. Something worse than rage: *clarity*.

**BPM:** 72 — the tempo of a slow walk. A procession. The pace at which you carry someone.

**Key:** E♭ minor throughout. No key change. No resolution. The harmony never arrives home, because home is what was taken.

**Time Signature:** 6/8 — a slow compound feel, the natural metre of lament, of rocking, of waiting rooms.

**Vocals:** Female alto, unadorned. No runs, no melisma, no vocal tricks. Sung the way you speak to someone who has stopped listening but you continue anyway, because the record must exist even if no one reads it. A voice that has cried already and is now past crying. Occasional backing vocals — three women, humming in close harmony beneath the verses like a congregation that knows the words but has learned there is no point in singing them loudly.

**Instrumentation:**
- **Harmonium** (or pump organ) — the primary instrument. Wheezing, imperfect, the sound of a church hall where the community meets because the hospital won't see them. Sustained chords, slow changes.
- **Single cello** — enters in verse 2. Long, bowed notes beneath the vocal. No melody of its own. It simply *holds*.
- **Djembe** — not a driving rhythm. A single, open-handed beat on 1 of each bar. A heartbeat. It stops during the bridge. Its absence is the point.
- **Acoustic guitar** — fingerpicked, sparse. Arrives in the final verse only. Three notes at a time. The sound of someone who still believes something can be built.
- **Backing vocals** — three women, humming. They do not sing words until the final chorus, when they sing one word each time: *"Heard."*

**Mood:** The exhausted dignity of someone who has done everything right — filed the forms, followed the process, used the correct language — and has been answered with silence shaped like procedure. Not angry. *Finished*. But still standing. Still singing. Because the song is the last form of evidence that cannot be filed away.

## Lyrics

### [Verse 1]
*(Harmonium alone. Voice enters unaccompanied for the first two lines, then harmonium beneath.)*

I came with what I knew.
I came with what I saw.
I brought it in my own two hands
and laid it at your door.

I used the proper channels.
I used the proper words.
I filled in every form you gave.
I trusted to be heard.

---

### [Pre-Chorus]
*(Backing vocals begin humming beneath. Low. A vibration more than a sound.)*

And you said —

---

### [Chorus]
*(Harmonium swells slightly. Cello enters, one long note.)*

"Thank you for your input.
Your concern has been received.
The matter is under review
and the process is believed

to be robust and thorough
and aligned with current goals."
…Meanwhile the bed is empty
where my mother sold her soul

for the promise of your care.

---

### [Verse 2]
*(Cello now present. Djembe heartbeat begins — one beat per bar.)*

You moved her in the winter
to a place without a name.
No windows and no medicine,
but the paperwork was clean.

I phoned. I wrote. I waited.
I stood outside your gate.
You said the process takes its course.
The process came too late.

---

### [Pre-Chorus]
*(Backing vocals slightly louder.)*

And you said —

---

### [Chorus]
*(Same arrangement. The repetition is deliberate — the system always says the same thing.)*

"Thank you for your input.
The incident was noted.
The committee met on Thursday
and the motion was promoted

to a framework for a review
of the standards that apply."
…But no framework in your filing cabinet
can tell me why she died.

Can you tell me why she died?

---

### [Bridge]
*(Everything drops out except harmonium, barely audible. The djembe stops. Silence where the heartbeat was. Spoken word, not sung — quiet, measured, the voice of testimony:)*

You did not lie to me.
That is what I can't forgive.

You did not lie.
You *processed* me.

You took my mother's death
and you made it
an agenda item.

You gave it a reference number.
You gave it a sub-committee.
You gave it everything
except a name.

Her name was Grace.

*(Two beats of silence.)*

---

### [Verse 3]
*(Acoustic guitar enters — fingerpicked, three notes. Cello returns. Djembe returns. The song rebuilds itself slowly, the way a person rebuilds after being disbelieved.)*

I know you'll call it "complex."
I know you'll call it "sad."
I know you'll say that lessons now
have certainly been had.

But lessons are for children,
and my mother was not young,
and the lesson that you taught me
is that nothing will be done

unless I sing it. So I'm singing.

---

### [Final Chorus]
*(Full arrangement. Backing vocals now sing the word "Heard" — once, on the downbeat of every other bar, like a demand, like a prayer, like a verdict.)*

Thank you for your input —
no. I'm done with being thanked.
I'm done with being "noted."
I'm done with being ranked

below your reputation,
below your bottom line,
below the quiet comfort
of your "everything is fine."

My mother deserved the truth.
She deserved it while alive.
Not a reference in a report
that arrived after she died.

---

### [Outro]
*(Instruments fall away one by one. Guitar first. Then cello. Then djembe — the heartbeat stops again. Harmonium alone. Voice alone.)*

The minutes will reflect…
that I was here.

The minutes will reflect…
that she was real.

*(Harmonium holds a single chord. It does not resolve. Fade to silence.)*

---

## Production Notes

- **The bridge must be completely still.** No reverb. No processing. A dry voice in a dry room saying a name. The name is the centre of the entire song.
- **The djembe stopping in the bridge and returning in verse 3** is the structural metaphor: a heartbeat that ceased and a life that continues to demand acknowledgement despite the silence.
- **The backing vocals singing "Heard"** in the final chorus should sound like both a statement and a question — *we were heard, weren't we? Weren't we?*
- **Do not add strings, pads, or any lushness.** The production should feel like a room with hard chairs and fluorescent lighting. The beauty must come entirely from the voice and the words. Anything more would be the musical equivalent of what the song is about — making suffering *comfortable*.
- **The song should end without resolution** — no final tonic chord, no fade to warmth. The harmonium holds a suspended chord and stops. Because the story hasn't ended. The process is still *under review*.
