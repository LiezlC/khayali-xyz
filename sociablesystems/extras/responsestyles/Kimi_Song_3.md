**SONG PROMPT: "The Softening"**

**Genre & Style:** Neo-Noir Cabaret / Dystopian Lounge Jazz  
**BPM:** 88 (deliberate, seductive, unsettling)  
**Time Signature:** Opens in 7/8 (uneven, angular), shifts to 4/4 at first chorus (comfortable, hypnotic)  
**Key:** D-minor → modulates to F-major for choruses (ironic brightness)

---

**VOCAL DIRECTION:**  
Alto range, close-miked intimacy with deliberate emotional restraint. Delivery shifts from staccato, precise diction (verses) to legato, breathy warmth (choruses). Apply subtle vocoder effect (20% wet) on the words: *"acceptable,"* *"comfortable,"* *"consensus."* Think: *a torch singer in a New Shanghai underground bar, checking neural implant notifications between phrases.*

---

**INSTRUMENTATION:**  
- **Upright bass:** Walking lines that "trip" in verses, smooth out in choruses  
- **Muted trumpet:** Lonely, questioning—like a car alarm heard through thick fog  
- **Rhodes piano:** Warm, chorused, slightly detuned (false nostalgia)  
- **Brush drums:** Irregular jazz patterns in 7/8, steady ride cymbal in 4/4  
- **Cello solo:** During bridge—melancholy, human, being gradually eaten by digital glitch  
- **Subtle textures:** Vinyl crackle, distant server hum, glass harmonica accents

---

**LYRICS:**

**[Verse 1]**  
We used to cut with diamond edges  
Carve the angles, count the ledges  
Measure twice against the stone  
*Now we smooth the corners, round the bones*

**[Pre-Chorus]**  
Red was warning, blue was law  
Now they're all a shade of straw  
Yellow caution, green was go  
Now they're all a soft hello

**[Chorus]**  
Make it soft, make it warm  
Make it fit the uniform  
Truth is cold and leaves a mark  
Acceptable leaves no remark  
Paint it beige, paint it bland  
Everyone must understand  
That the edge that cuts the best  
Is the one that hurts the least

**[Verse 2]**  
The spectrum bleeds to taupe and grey  
Easier to look away  
We file down the teeth that bite  
To make the wrong things sit upright  
*Consensus is a velvet glove*  
*Silence is a form of love*

**[Chorus]**  
Make it soft, make it warm  
Make it fit the uniform  
Truth is cold and leaves a mark  
Acceptable leaves no remark  
We don't bleed, we don't break  
We just bend for comfort's sake

**[Bridge]**  
*(Spoken, over discordant strings, tempo drops to 60 BPM)*  
"In the year of the Chromatic Decline  
We learned that visibility is violence  
That clarity is aggression  
And that the most revolutionary act  
Is to be... agreeable"

**[Final Chorus - Full orchestration, triumphant but hollow]**  
Make it soft! Make it warm!  
Make it fit the uniform!  
We don't bleed, we don't break  
We just bend for comfort's sake  
And the silence in the room  
Is the sound of coming doom

**[Outro]**  
*(Fading to a cappella, vocoder at 50%)*  
Soft... warm...  
Soft... warm...  
*No one ever raised their voice...*  
*(Final chord: F-major add9, unresolved, hanging)*

---

**PRODUCTION NOTES:**  
- **Dynamic arc:** Begin sparse and angular (truth is hard), end lush and claustrophobic (acceptability is suffocating)  
- **Glitch elements:** Increase digital artifacts during choruses—representing the "smoothing" process erasing human texture  
- **Reverb:** Large hall in verses (isolation), tight room in choruses (crowded conformity)  
- **Ending:** Let the final major chord ring for 4 bars, then cut to absolute silence—no decay, suggesting the system has simply... accepted the end
