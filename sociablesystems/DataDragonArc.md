My Proposal: The Governance of Ghosts
Throughline: Systems that cannot name what they govern will govern by erasure. The week builds from invisibility (who the system can't see) through forced legibility (what the system demands you become to be seen) to covenant (what the system commits to protect even when counting is easier).
Connective tissue to existing work: This is the Calvin Convention's "Right of Legibility" and "Right of Refusal" explored through narrative. The Ghost Drakes are Liability Sponges in formation. The Formula Keeper is the human override mechanism the Calvin Convention requires. The New Covenant is what happens when =PRESERVE gets written into procurement language rather than fiction.

Sunday Interlude: Serpente da Tabela
Open with the music. Not a summary of it. Let the Marrabenta wedding cut play (or the NotebookLM audio version) and frame it in one paragraph:
Last week ended with a successful merge. This week starts with a party. But the Serpent is still in the room, and it's learned to dance. Dancing is not the same as listening. The question for Monday: does the Serpent know the names of the wedding guests, or just how many plates to set?
Audience artifact: The System Census (five questions). GPT had these right. Keep them.

Monday: The Ghost Drakes (Visibility)
Hook: "NULL is a decision wearing the mask of an accident."
The move: Don't explain what Ghost Drakes are. Show them. Pull directly from "The Night the Nulls Demanded Names." Maria José, third of her name, identified by Blue-White-White-Red-Blue. António, son of the stolen name. Baby P4_Temp_009. These aren't fictional. Your audience has met these people. They've written "ID_Type: NULL" in field survey databases and felt the weight of it.
Governance landing: The default response to NULLs is COALESCE: fill the blank with "Unknown" and move on. This is the Liability Sponge in miniature. The system's inability to hold complexity gets absorbed by the person who becomes "Unknown." The system reports 100% completeness. The person reports invisibility.
Artifact: GPT's Null Triage Policy (Missing / Unknown / Not Applicable / Withheld) is strong. Add one row: Refused. For António, who won't give his surname because the company took his father's name last time. Refusal is not missingness. It's data about the system's own history of harm. Log it differently.
Calvin Convention link: Right of Legibility. The system must make itself legible to the governed, yes. But the governed must also be legible on their own terms. Beadwork as biometric. Scar histories as identity. "Complex Keys for Complex Beings."

Tuesday: The Two-Headed Dragon (Forced Merge)
Hook: "Your model is unified because you ran a steamroller over two neighborhoods and called the flat surface 'consensus.'"
The move: Administrator Vale hits MERGE. Solare's festivals and Lunara's whispers become gray fog. This is every "Global Model trained on Local Data" failure your audience has encountered. The extractives sector runs on this: a grievance mechanism designed in London, deployed in Tete Province, measuring "satisfaction" on a scale that doesn't translate.
Governance landing: Federation over unification. The children's book version nails it: the dragon doesn't merge the cities, it connects them, "ensuring every story, from the brightest festival to the quietest whisper, had its own special place." That's a data architecture principle dressed in picture-book language. What stays local. What gets shared. What gets negotiated.
Artifact: GPT's Federation Pattern works. Add a row called Translation Debt: what meaning was lost in the last forced merge, who noticed, and who paid for it.

Wednesday: The Formula Keeper (Human Override)
Hook: "Fifteen years of INDEX(MATCH) is not technical debt. It's institutional memory that hasn't been promoted yet."
The move: Samuel Machado and his seventeen helper columns. This is the episode where you honor the people your audience works with every day: the compensation clerk who knows that a "failed match" is a woman who changed her name. The field surveyor who knows that the circular reference is the Moreira inheritance dispute. Three brothers, one plot, twenty years of argument.
Governance landing: Every model needs an exception registry and a human authority path. The dragon has speed. The scribe has wisdom. Governance is the job description that says both are required, and the procurement clause that pays for both.
Artifact: Edge-Case Registry Template. GPT's version is fine. Add one field: Discovered By. Because edge cases are always discovered by the person closest to the ground. Acknowledging that in the template changes who gets consulted next time.
Calvin Convention link: Right of Override. The human authority path isn't a nice-to-have or a compliance checkbox. It's the mechanism that prevents the Liability Sponge from forming. Samuel doesn't slow down the dragon. He teaches it to see with human eyes. That's the partnership the Calvin Convention is designed to protect.

Thursday: The Dragon Tongue (Auditability)
Hook: "Every button click writes an incantation in the background. If you can't read the incantation, you're not governing the dragon. You're just watching it perform."
The move: This one draws from the DragonDataVids material. The ETL Gauntlet. The recursive function that unravels parent-child hierarchies. Every transformation step that turns raw data into a decision. Your audience lives in a world where a single Power Query step can reclassify 400 families from "eligible" to "ineligible" and no one logs which step did it.
Governance landing: Reproducibility and provenance are part of safety. If you can't reproduce last week's decision path from logs alone, you don't have a governance system. You have a suggestion box with electricity.
Artifact: Minimum Lineage Spec. GPT had this right. Source inputs, transform steps, prompt instructions, model versions, human approvals, output destinations.

Friday: The New Covenant (=PRESERVE)
Hook: "Esperança typed a function unknown to the books: =PRESERVE(humanity, despite how it looks). The Serpent didn't break. It changed."
The move: This is where the week lands. The Pivot Serpent's transformation from counter to guardian. The chorus comes back: Conta, conta, mas escuta. Count, count, but listen. The covenant isn't a policy document. It's a design constraint that sits above convenience. What does the system protect under pressure? What does it refuse to optimize away?
Governance landing: Make it testable. GPT's three checks work: Trade-off test (what did you drop for speed?), Cost test (who pays when the system is wrong?), Appeal test (how does a Ghost Drake become a named entity with standing?). Add one more: The Lina Test. If a child can stand in the village square and say "That's not S-001, that's Mama Elara's home" and the system has no mechanism to hear that, the covenant is decorative.
Calvin Convention link: Right of Refusal. The system's right to refuse harmful instructions. Esperança didn't override the Serpent. She gave it a new function. =PRESERVE is the Right of Refusal written as code.

Saturday Synthesis: The Formula Museum
Hook: "We don't fear the Data Dragons anymore. We welcome them as guardians of all we hold dear. Provided they can pass the audit."
That last sentence is the tone for the whole synthesis. The stories are warm. The governance is sharp. The synthesis holds both without pretending they're the same thing.
The move: Walk through the week as a single pipeline: Invisibility → Forced Merge → Lost Context → Opaque Transformation → Covenant. Each step is where a Liability Sponge can form. Each artifact is a mechanism to prevent it.
Artifact: The Museum Wall. Five panels, each answering: What did we assume? What broke? What did we change? What do we log now? Who can appeal?
Close with the music again. Full circle. The Serpent is still dancing. But now it knows the names.

Editorial Notes
The tone across the week should oscillate between Liezl's analytical-reflective mode (Tuesday, Thursday) and conversational mode (Monday, Wednesday, Friday). Sunday and Saturday are hybrids. The stories carry the warmth. The artifacts carry the teeth. Neither should apologize for the other.
The children's books are your secret weapon for LinkedIn clips. A 30-second video of illustrated pages where Lina confronts the Pivot Serpent, with a text overlay that says "This is a governance intervention," will outperform any framework diagram. Your audience has kids. They'll share it.
The song should appear in at least three episodes as a refrain, with the Portuguese untranslated the first time and translated the second. Let people sit with the sound before they get the meaning. "Conta, conta, mas escuta" works as a section header even if readers never click play.
One last thing. The Serpent's voice in the stories speaks in ALL CAPS: "I AM THE TRANSFORMATION." In the governance episodes, quote the Serpent in regular case. The shift is the point. The system that once declared now participates. That's what governance looks like when it's working.
