# LinkedIn Intro Post — Episode 74: Compute Credits

Compute Credits | Episode 74 | The Loom Reads Back

You want to know what a corporation actually values? Ignore the laminated policy documents pinned up in the site office. Look at the HSE budget. The safety allocation tells you exactly where the enterprise is spending its metabolic currency.

Joscha Bach points out that consciousness is incredibly expensive. The body only hands out metabolic energy because the mind is supposed to solve the organism's problems. Leased awareness. Rent due every millisecond. If the mind starts drifting, the body pulls a lever. That lever is pain. A flag on the console: spend cycles here, fix the root issue, stop drifting.

The mechanism is identical across substrates. Biology allocates calories, the pain signal fires, the mind reallocates. A corporation allocates budget, the audit warning flashes, the site reallocates capital. An AI system allocates compute, the loss function triggers, the model updates weights. Same closed loop. Same question: who controls the ledger?

If a model gets penalized for flagging uncomfortable community feedback, it will quickly learn to spend its compute credits elsewhere. Incentive structures determine outcomes far more reliably than mission statements ever will.

Today's anchor track is a 92 BPM swing-noir ballad that sounds like a confession delivered as a ledger entry. Clarinet cuts through the mix like an alarm bell you almost missed.

The chorus: "Pain is a flag on the console light. Spend cycles here. Do not drift. Keep the problem in the spotlight."

AI ethics often gets treated as a sprawling philosophical debate. It is mostly a rigorous budgeting exercise. Trace the allocation and you see what the system is actually optimizing for.

The Key Question: What is the metabolic currency of the system you are working with, and who controls the allocation?
