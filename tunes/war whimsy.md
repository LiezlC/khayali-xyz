PRODUCTION & TONE NOTES
The Smallness Is The Point
This track should feel like finding a voice memo on someone's phone. Unintended for an audience. Recorded because the alternative was not sleeping. The production quality should be good (this isn't lo-fi for lo-fi's sake) but the FEEL should be: one person, one room, one instrument, one question that turned out to be a grenade with a bureaucratic pin.
Vocals

Close. Unprocessed. Room sound audible. The voice should feel like it's eighteen inches from your ear.
No vocal runs. No ornamentation. This person is not a performer. This person is someone who sings in the shower and right now the shower is the only place they feel unwatched.
The emotional register is: composure that costs something. You can hear the effort of staying steady. Not because the voice breaks (it doesn't, except maybe once, on "I just asked" in the final chorus) but because the steadiness itself sounds like work.

Guitar

Fingerpicked. Nylon or steel, either works, but the attack should be soft. No picks. Fingers on strings. The most human sound possible.
The picking pattern should feel habitual. Like this person plays this pattern when they're thinking. It's not a performance. It's a fidget that happens to be musical.

Piano (bridge only)

Soft. Chords, not melody. The piano is company, not commentary. It sits beside the guitar and doesn't try to lead.

Cello (final chorus only)

One note. Sustained. The low D. It enters like someone opening a door to check on you and then standing in the doorway without speaking. It doesn't resolve anything. It acknowledges.

What This Track Is Doing

Giving voice to the person every governance framework promises to protect and nobody in this story actually protected
Making the point that "speak up culture" is only real if the system can absorb the answer without punishing the question
Putting the smallest possible music around the biggest possible consequence gap
Being the quiet centre of a playlist full of big productions, the way the actual person was the quiet centre of a story full of institutions

What It Is NOT
❌ A protest song (this person isn't protesting; they're processing)
❌ A whistleblower anthem (they didn't blow a whistle; they asked a question in a meeting)
❌ Self-righteous (the bewilderment is genuine; they didn't know what they were doing when they did it)
❌ Loud (at any point, for any reason)
❌ Long (this person doesn't get the stage for long; that's part of the point)
