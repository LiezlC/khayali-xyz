“D.I. Dimes” — a Budget Blues (with Groove)

[PRONUNCIATION NOTE FOR ALL VOCALS]
“D.I.” is pronounced “Dee Eye” (two letters, spelled out).
Do NOT pronounce as “die.” Think initials, not a word.

INTRO

[Sound: coin drop. Spreadsheet click. Calculator beep. Deep, steady bass.]

D.I. voice (neutral, polite):
“Welcome. I will now optimise your finances.”

Human voice (female, calm, wry):
“Okay. Let’s take a look.”

(Note: no sarcasm, no eye-roll. This sets authority, not rebellion.)

VERSE 1 — THE PROMISE

[Laid-back afro-house groove, 112 BPM]

D.I. Dimes speaks clear and neat,
Budgets, patterns, lines that meet,
“Track your spend, reduce the strain,
Future-you will thank you — gain by gain.”

It scans my app, pauses a beat,
Says “Some decisions repeat.”
“Daily coffee noted here.”
Yes. That’s the margin where I steer.

(Shift from “coping” to “choosing”)

PRE-CHORUS

[Reggae skank, restrained]

D.I., D.I.—
Always ready to reply,
D.I., D.I.—
Sees the numbers, not the why.

CHORUS

[Full groove, grounded, assured]

D.I. Dimes — (Dee Eye!)
Says my budget’s misaligned,
But dignity don’t live in cells,
And life’s not built on tables well.
You can chart my cents and plans,
Run projections, scan my hands,
But context doesn’t show in rows—
That’s something D.I. still don’t know.

(Chorus lands as statement, not protest)

VERSE 2 — THE REALITY CHECK

[Electro-swing stabs, observational]

“Cancel streaming,” D.I. said,
“Save eighty-nine rand instead.”
Eighty-nine rand won’t change the ground,
But silence sometimes keeps me sound.

Taxi fare flagged. Groceries too.
“Cook at home” — as if that’s new.
After work, after care, after days that bend,
Convenience is sometimes how I defend my end.

A voice inside says, measured, clear:
“I know my limits. I was here.”
Some budgets stretch, some budgets stand,
Some hold the line the best they can.

(Internal authority replaces external validation)

BRIDGE — THE TRUTH

[Drop to warm pad, reflective]

D.I. knows the cost of things,
Interest curves, tightening rings,
But slows when trying to account
For care that never shows an amount.

Helping family breaks the rule,
Still keeps the world from turning cruel,
“Emergency fund” looks clean on screen,
Less so where the margins live unseen.

FINAL CHORUS

[Gospel-house lift, collective strength]

D.I. Dimes — (Dee Eye!)
You’re a tool, not a guide,
Help me plan, help me see
What’s possible responsibly.
But don’t frame survival wrong
As weakness when it took so long,
Teach me tools, not guilt or shame—
