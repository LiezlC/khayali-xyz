D.I. : DYSTOPIA
Theme Song Prompt
Afro-Tech / Deep House / Singalong Minimalism
MUSICAL INTENT

This track should work in three places at once:

a packed dancefloor

a late-night drive

the back of someone’s mind the next morning

It sounds joyful.
It means something else.

GENRE & GROOVE

Primary: Afro-Tech / Deep House
Tempo: 123 BPM
Feel: dark-jolly, hypnotic, confident, infectious
Swing: subtle, human, undeniable

Think:

Black Coffee restraint

Be Svendsen darkness with bounce

Oceanvs Orientalis mysticism

Tech house clarity, not aggression

CORE IDEA (VERY SIMPLE)

Dystopia isn’t flames and rubble.
It’s panic dressed as protection.
It’s rules multiplying faster than understanding.

D.I. notices this.
D.I. dances anyway.

STRUCTURE (5–6 MIN)
Section	Energy	Purpose
Intro	30%	Set the calm
Groove Lock	55%	Pull bodies in
Hook Drop	85%	Truth lands
Mid-Groove	70%	Hypnosis
Final Drop	90%	Sing it together
Outro	40%	Loop continues
INTRO (0:00–0:45)

Warm kick.
Rolling bass.
No drama.

Filtered vocal, almost casual:

Status check…
All systems protected


Let that irony breathe.

MAIN HOOK (THE HEART OF THE TRACK)

This is the entire lyrical spine.
Everything else supports it.

PRIMARY CHANT (ULTRA SINGABLE)
Safe
Safe
Safe

Still afraid


That’s it.
That’s the hook.

Call-and-response works beautifully here:

Lead: Safe

Crowd: Safe

Crowd again: Safe

Drop: Still afraid

GROOVE DROP (1:30–2:30)

Full Afro-tech groove hits.
Bass walks confidently.
Percussion swings.

Secondary vocal line, looped:

Built it to protect us
Locked it from ourselves


Keep it rhythmic, not emotional.

MID-TRACK VARIATION (2:30–3:30)

Strip back slightly.
Add melodic texture (strings or sax fragment).

New line, almost whispered:

Dystopia
Doesn’t scream
It reassures


Let “reassures” stretch and echo.

FINAL DROP (3:30–5:00)

Bring the crowd back in.

FULL CHANT, BIG ENERGY
Safe
Safe
Safe

Still afraid

Guarded
Managed
Measured

Still afraid


Background vocals repeat “Still afraid”
until it becomes rhythm, not meaning.

OUTRO (5:00–end)

Elements peel away.

Last spoken D.I. line, dry and calm:

Observation complete
Protection did not reduce fear


Kick continues for a few bars.
Fade.

PRODUCTION NOTES (IMPORTANT)
Vocals

Few words, maximum clarity

Chantable by drunk humans at 2am

No soul runs, no drama

Neutral voice, close mic

Rhythm

Dancefloor first, always

Groove must feel good, not heavy

Sub-bass warm and friendly

Texture

Slightly ominous pads underneath

Nothing cold or sci-fi

This dystopia wears a smile

WHAT THIS TRACK DOES (WITHOUT EXPLAINING IT)

• Exposes fear-based governance without naming it
• Makes “safety panic” absurd by repetition
• Turns critique into a chant
• Lets people sing the problem before they think it

FINAL GENERATION PROMPT (SHORT FORM)

Create an Afro-tech / deep house track at ~123 BPM that is ultra danceable and singalong-ready. Minimal lyrics, chant-based. Core hook: “Safe / Safe / Safe / Still afraid.” Theme: modern dystopia as fear disguised as protection. Warm bass, human swing, dark-jolly mood. No aggression, no sentimentality, no exposition. Let repetition carry the message.
