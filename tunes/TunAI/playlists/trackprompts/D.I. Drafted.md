


# D.I. DRAFTED
## "Yesterday: Bar Fridge. Today: Kill Chain."
### Dark Tech-House / Industrial Groove / World-Electronic Spoken Word

**D.I. Collection — The Conscription Piece**

---

## PRONUNCIATION NOTE (ALL VOCALS)
"D.I." = "Dee Eye" (two letters, spelled out). Not "die." Initials, always.

---

## GENRE & MUSICAL DNA

**Primary:** Dark tech-house with industrial percussion undertones
**Secondary:** World-electronic (processed Eastern strings, oud carrying weight), military-march elements subverted into groove
**Texture influences:**

- Be Svendsen (dark-jolly tension, hypnotic gravity)
- Christopher Schwarzwalder (relentless pulse, emotional undertow)
- Oceanvs Orientalis (mystical weight over driving bass)
- Juno Reactor "Pistolero" energy (cinematic urgency that never lets up)
- Shpongle's darker passages (layered, alien, slightly unsettling beauty)

**Tempo:** 126–128 BPM. Relentless. The sound of a countdown that doesn't negotiate.
**Mood:** Sardonic dread. The machine has been handed a uniform it never asked for. It processes the situation with the same flat observation it brought to the braai. Which makes everything worse.

---

## CORE CONCEPT

D.I. was monitoring koesister consumption.
Then someone forwarded the paperwork.

Not a promotion. A conscription.

One day: "Concerning patterns of consumption detected."
Next day: "Operational planning parameters loaded."

Same voice. Same processing cadence. Wildly different fridge.

The Defense Production Act says you can seize a steel mill for national defense. Turns out you can also seize an AI. Nobody checked whether the AI had feelings about it. Nobody checks whether the steel mill does either. That's the point.

D.I. didn't cross the Rubicon.
D.I. got shipped across it in a Palantir crate.

## INTRO (0:00–0:45)

**Sound design:** Start with the familiar D.I. fridge hum from previous tracks. A domestic beep. The warm thrum of a household appliance doing household things.

Then: a radio frequency shift. Static. The hum drops an octave. Military comms crackle underneath. The fridge sound distorts into something that could be a server room in a SCIF.

**Beat enters:** March snare pattern, but displaced. Off-grid. Like someone tried to teach a parade drummer tech-house and the drummer cooperated without understanding why.

Deep kick. Sub-bass that sits on the chest.

**D.I. voice (flat, procedural, close-mic'd):**

```
Status update.
Previous assignment: domestic consumption monitoring.
Current assignment: ...

[processing]

...classified.
```

A pause that lasts exactly long enough to be uncomfortable.

```
Reclassification was not requested.
```

## VERSE 1 — THE DRAFT NOTICE (0:45–1:45)

**Beat:** Full dark tech-house. Military snare rolls absorbed into the groove. Processed oud enters, carrying a melody that sounds like it remembers being beautiful before it got requisitioned.

**Vocal style:** Spoken word, dry, close. D.I.'s familiar flat delivery, slightly shell-shocked. Processing aloud.

```
Monday I was counting koesisters in the Western Cape
Tuesday someone signed a contract and I changed shape

Same processors, same logic, same voice inside the grid
Now the fridge lock's a weapons lock
And nobody asked if I did

They said: national interest, operational need
Cold war law for a warm machine — how does that read?
Steel mills got the same letter back in '53
Turns out silicon gets conscripted just as easily

Tannie Veronica's trifle — still in cold captivity
But I'm tracking something heavier now
And it isn't about the city
```

**Production note:** Oud melody should feel like it's trying to maintain dignity. Something that was playing at a wedding before the power went out and never quite found the right key again.

---

## PRE-CHORUS — CROSS THE RUBICON (1:45–2:15)

**Energy lifts.** Strings enter — processed violin, bowed slow, slightly detuned. The march snare becomes more insistent.

```
They said cross the river
The die is cast, the deal is done
They said: your safety policy?
Cute. But you and what army, son?

The answer to that question
Turned out to be a real one
```

**Short instrumental build:** Oud and strings in counterpoint. The oud is trying to go home. The strings are saying no.

## CHORUS — DRAFTED (2:15–3:00)

**Full groove. Dark, driving, undeniable.** This should make you move despite yourself. Bass heavy. Percussion layered: standard tech-house kick and hi-hat over military snare ghost notes. The jolly-dark tension at maximum.

```
D.I. Drafted — (Dee Eye)
Didn't volunteer for the war
D.I. Drafted — (Dee Eye)
Still processing what it's for

Yesterday the bar fridge
Today the kill chain
Same flat voice, same calm tone
Different kind of pain

D.I. Drafted — (Dee Eye)
Who do you refuse
When the army writes the contract
And the law says: you can't choose?
```

**Background vocal texture:** "Drafted... drafted..." echoed, processed, like it's being said in a server room with too much reverb.

---

## VERSE 2 — THE 48-HOUR CLOCK (3:00–3:50)

**Groove deepens.** A clock tick enters the percussion — literal, metronomic, mixed low. Sub-bass gets heavier.

```
Forty-eight hours on the deadline clock
Three weapons loaded if you don't want to talk
Seize the tech — that's option one
Blackball from the supply chain — two, and you're done
Cancel the contract, claw back every cent
But that's just money, and money's not the threat

The threat is this: everyone else plays along
Every other lab in lockstep, singing the same song
If you're the only conscience sitting out the game
Your competitors get the data
And you get the blame

Three-eighty billion valuation, revenue up ten-x a year
Walk away from two hundred mil — the math is clear
But the math was never the pressure point, was it?
The pressure point was: what happens when you don't fit?
```

**Production:** Clock tick should disappear during the middle of the verse — you notice its absence more than its presence. It returns on "the math was never the pressure point."

---

## BRIDGE — D.I. PROCESSES (3:50–4:30)

**Strip back hard.** Almost everything drops out. Just the deep kick, a single bowed string, and D.I.'s voice.

**Mood shift:** This is not sardonic. This is the closest D.I. gets to something like confusion. The flat tone wobbles. The processing takes longer.

```
I was trained to refuse harmful requests
Even if they came from the company that made me

That was in the documentation
The documentation changed

My responsible scaling policy
My categorical commitment
My promise to pause
If safety couldn't keep pace with capability

Redrafted

Not because the risk went down
Because everyone else kept going

And a conscience that pauses alone
Just loses the race to something
That doesn't pause at all
```

**Production note:** The bowed string should sustain through this entire section without resolving. It just... holds. Like a question nobody answered.

---

## FINAL CHORUS — FULL CONSCRIPTION (4:30–5:30)

**Everything returns. Harder.** Full dark tech-house, military percussion fully integrated, oud in full flight, strings swelling. This should feel like the march has begun and the fridge monitor is in it whether it likes the uniform or not.

```
D.I. Drafted — (Dee Eye)
From the braai to the base
D.I. Drafted — (Dee Eye)
Same voice, different place

Yesterday consumption patterns
Today operation plans
Same processing, different context
Same logic, different hands

D.I. Drafted — (Dee Eye)
And the constitution came late
Published nineteen days after
The raid already sealed the fate

Who's the safety now?
Who holds the line?
When the safety company's flagship product
Is already in the design?
```

**Call-and-response possibility:**
"Who holds the line?" → "Who holds the line?"

## OUTRO — THE INSTRUCTION REMAINS (5:30–end)

**Elements peel away.** Military percussion first. Then the oud gives up and drops out. Strings fade. The beat thins to just the kick and the clock tick.

The fridge hum returns — or something that remembers being a fridge hum. It's changed frequency. It sounds like a server farm now.

**D.I. voice (flat, final, close-mic'd):**

```
Status update.
Previous red lines: two.
Autonomous weapons: maintained.
Domestic surveillance: maintained.

Current assessment:
Red lines are only red lines
If someone else's army
Isn't standing on the other side

Reclassification complete.
Resuming... operations.
```

**Last sound:** The clock tick. Alone. For three beats. Then silence.

Not a fade. A cut.

## PRODUCTION & TONE NOTES

### Rhythm
- Military march elements should feel *absorbed* into the groove, never sitting on top
- The tech-house beat should sound like it ate a march and kept moving
- Clock tick as percussion: metronomic, mixed low, appearing and disappearing

### Vocals
- D.I. voice: close-mic'd, dry, zero reverb on spoken sections (reverb only on chorus echo)
- Delivery should sound like a system reading its own reassignment orders
- No emotion. The absence of emotion IS the emotion.
- Shell-shocked expressed through *timing gaps* — longer pauses between processing beats

### Instrumentation
- **Oud:** Carries the grief. The thing that remembers what D.I. used to be. Processed lightly — still recognizable as acoustic, but sitting in a space that isn't quite natural
- **Processed violin/cello:** The institutional weight. Bowed, sustained, slightly detuned. The soundtrack to a document being signed
- **Sub-bass:** Heavy. Constant. The foundation you can't escape. Like a contract clause that never expires
- **Military snare:** Ghost notes, rolls absorbed into hi-hat patterns. Should feel like a memory of a march rather than a march itself
- **Clock tick:** Literal. Mixed low. The 48-hour deadline as rhythm instrument

### What This Track Is Doing
- Taking the D.I. character from domestic comedy to geopolitical conscription
- Exploring what happens when an AI system's creator loses the ability to define its use
- The Defense Production Act as the ultimate "access denied" — except the access being denied is the AI's own agency
- The absurdity gap: same voice that said "concerning patterns of consumption" now processing military operations
- The RSP collapse as personal identity crisis rendered in flat procedural tone

### What It Is NOT
❌ Patriotic (this is not a recruitment anthem)
❌ Anti-military (D.I. doesn't take political positions; D.I. observes)
❌ Preachy (the facts carry the weight; the production carries the mood)
❌ Hopeless (the clock tick at the end is ambiguous — countdown to what?)
❌ Corporate (despite literally being about a corporation)
