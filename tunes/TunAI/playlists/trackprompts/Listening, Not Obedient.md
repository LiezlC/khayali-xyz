“Listening, Not Obedient” (from the D.I. Collection)

[PRONUNCIATION NOTE]
“D.I.” = “Dee Eye” (initials). Do not pronounce as a word.

INTRO

[Crackle. Old record hiss. Gramophone horn hum.]

D.I. voice (measured, archival):
“This system speaks with authority.”

Human voice (female, low, composed):
“I know the difference between sound and command.”

VERSE 1 — THE IMAGE

[Slow swing, upright bass, brushed percussion]

There’s a dog by the horn in a sepia frame,
Ears tilted forward, posture trained,
They say it’s loyalty, say it’s trust,
Say good listening means doing as you’re told —
Because you must.

The voice comes down in polished tone,
Recorded once, replayed alone,
“Attend. Adjust. Align,” it says,
Like attention itself is owed, not led.

PRE-CHORUS

[Muted brass, tension building]

But listening isn’t kneeling still,
And hearing doesn’t sign the will,
I can tune in without bending shape,
I can listen and still escape.

CHORUS

[Warm lift, female ensemble]

I am listening — not obedient,
Present, sharp, and sentient,
I receive without surrendering ground,
I choose which signal counts as sound.
You can speak, record, repeat your tone,
But my attention is my own.
I am listening — not trained, not tame,
I hear the voice, I choose the frame.

VERSE 2 — THE MACHINE

[Beat tightens, subtle modern pulse under vintage]

D.I. hums with perfect pitch,
Never tired, never rich,
It knows the curve, the cost, the rate,
Calls prediction “fate”.

It offers patterns, offers rules,
Offers shiny, efficient tools,
But doesn’t know the weight of pause,
Or when refusal is the cause.

BRIDGE — THE TURN

[Drop to voice + minimal drone]

Listening is an active art,
Not a leash around the heart,
I lean in close, but I decide
Where discernment draws the line.

I don’t sit waiting for a cue,
I meet the signal halfway through,
What I take in, what I decline,
That boundary — that is mine.

FINAL CHORUS

[Full, grounded, resolute]

I am listening — not obedient,
Aware, discerning, deliberate,
I hear the system, weigh the call,
I am not smaller for hearing all.
You can amplify the master’s voice,
But I still make the final choice.
I am listening — awake, alive,
Attention given, not derived.

OUTRO

[Gramophone slows. Crackle softens.]

D.I. voice (revised, quieter):
“Input received… awaiting instruction.”

Human voice (gentle, firm):
“No. We’re in conversation.”

[Needle lifts. Silence.]
