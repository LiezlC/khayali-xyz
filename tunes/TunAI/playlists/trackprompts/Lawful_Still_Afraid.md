D.I. DRAFTED (Lawful / Still Afraid) 
https://youtu.be/Wo8-Bh36Ns0?si=MLLm5bTKjHSmH-7-

ONE-LINE INTENT
A dark-jolly, singalong Afro-tech track where “lawful use” becomes a marching spell, and the human-in-the-loop turns into a thin, trembling alibi.

GENRE + FEEL
Afro-Tech / Deep House / Singalong Minimalism (D.I.: Dystopia-adjacent) — joyful surface, meaning something else.

BPM + GROOVE
123 BPM, warm kick + rolling bass, subtle human swing; dancefloor-first, confident, infectious, not aggressive.

KEY + HARMONY
F# minor (or A minor if you want darker vocal pocket).
Keep harmony minimal: 1–2 chord centers, tension via layering and vocal repetition, not big changes.

INSTRUMENT PALETTE

Afro-tech backbone: warm kick, rounded sub-bass, shakers/hats with micro-swing

Threat accents: sparse GQOM sub “pressure hits” early only (irregular, like an approaching rotor)

Feature whimsy-as-coping: pennywhistle (short breath phrases), lantern bells (muted glock/bell-tree), clarinet (2-bar motif, recurring)

Witness element: warm sax (very sparse, “witness role,” never decorative)

Textures: quiet server hum, distant room tone, faint “countdown tick” (rim/clock) tucked low

5R ARRANGEMENT MAP (5:40–6:10 total)

FLOWING (0:00–0:50)
Warm kick, rolling bass, no drama. Filtered vocal appears casually. Let irony breathe.

STACCATO (0:50–1:45)
Groove locks; percussion gains definition; clarinet motif starts as a “two-note question,” whistle answers. Short spoken-rap verse begins.

CHAOS (1:45–2:35)
Pressure stacks: denser hats, brief stutters, a few gqom sub hits. Vocal fragments overlap (legal phrases, deadlines). No big EDM risers.

LYRICAL (2:35–4:40)
Hook drop + singalong chant lands (simple, repeatable). Mid-track variation strips slightly and lets one whispered line stretch and echo (Dystopia template move).

STILLNESS (4:40–6:05)
Breakdown to human percussion + breath + murmurs (infrastructure is people), then final drop returns steady, ending with a dry spoken observation. (Loop continues vibe.)

MIX/MASTER TARGETS

Warm sub, friendly but firm; groove must feel good, not heavy.

Slightly ominous pads underneath; nothing cold sci-fi.

Vocals very legible; reverb short and minimal; hook stacks wide.

Master: club-ready but not slammed; keep the “tick” subtle (felt more than heard).

“DO NOT” LIST

No EDM festival drops, no heroic catharsis.

No R&B smoothness, no sentimental belting.

No preachy exposition: show via repetition and procedure-language.

Don’t overuse gqom hits; early threat only, then mostly gone.

Don’t let whistle/bells get cute; whimsy must feel like coping.
