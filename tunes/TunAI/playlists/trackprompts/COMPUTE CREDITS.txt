TRACK PROMPT: “COMPUTE CREDITS”
Genre and vibe

Vintage swing-noir ballad with a subtle modern pulse.
Think late-night jazz club, cigarette-smoke shadows, but with a faint “machine-room” undertone hiding under the brass.

Mood: cool, controlled, slightly haunted.
This is not melodrama. It is a confession delivered like a ledger entry.

Tempo, meter

92 BPM

Swing feel (triplet swing), 4/4

Key and harmony

D minor (primary)

Brief lift to F major for one chorus turnaround, then back to D minor

Jazz voicings: minor 6, minor 9, half-diminished passing chords, occasional tritone substitution on turnarounds

Core sonic palette

Upright bass, brushed drums, piano (no bright pop piano, keep it woody)

Muted trumpet for punctuation phrases

Clarinet as the signature “alarm bell” motif

Optional: very low, near-inaudible sub-hum to imply a server room, but keep it subtle so it does not turn into sci-fi

Vocal approach

Intimate, close-mic

Spoken-sung delivery in verses (like a noir monologue that occasionally turns melodic)

Choruses more melodic, still restrained

Optional harmony: a second voice enters only in the last chorus, soft and ghostlike, as if the system is arguing with itself

Arrangement and structure

Intro (0:00–0:15)
Piano alone, slow noir chords. Clarinet hints at a 4-note motif, like a warning you almost miss.

Verse 1 (0:15–0:50)
Brushes and upright bass enter. Vocal is half-spoken, precise.

Chorus (0:50–1:15)
Muted trumpet answers the vocal lines. Clarinet motif becomes clearer.

Verse 2 (1:15–1:55)
Slightly denser piano comping, still sparse. Add small cymbal swells.

Chorus (1:55–2:20)
Same as first, but with a small harmonic lift near the end.

Bridge (2:20–2:55)
Drop drums to just brushes on snare rim. Vocal becomes more intimate and dangerous. Clarinet turns slightly dissonant, like a bent note that will not resolve.

Final Chorus (2:55–3:25)
Full ensemble returns. Optional second voice harmony. End on a D minor chord that holds, then a single clarinet note fades.

Mix notes

Keep room tone. Make it feel recorded in a small club.

Bass warm and present, not boomy.

Vocal upfront, breath audible.

Clarinet should cut through like an attention signal, but never become “cute.”
