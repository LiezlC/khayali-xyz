## NOTES ON SOUTH AFRICAN AGRICULTURAL LENDING

**Typical Terms for Farm-Secured Facilities (2026):**
- **Interest Rates**: Prime + 0% to Prime + 2% (approximately 12.25%–14.25% as of March 2026)
- **LTV (Loan-to-Value)**: Banks typically lend 50–70% of property value for agricultural properties
- **Term**: 24–60 months for working capital; longer for infrastructure
- **Repayment Frequency**: Monthly installments standard; quarterly available for seasonal operations
- **Insurance Requirements**: Property insurance (compulsory); life/disability insurance may be required

**Advantages of Farm-Secured vs. Unsecured:**
- Lower interest rates (5–7% cheaper than personal loans or overdraft extensions)
- Longer terms reduce monthly payment burden
- Professional positioning: banks respect asset-backed borrowing
- Tax considerations: interest on property-secured facilities may be more favorably treated

**Challenges to Anticipate:**
- Banks may require farm valuation (cost: R 2,000–R 8,000; typically bank-appointed)
- Title transfer fees and legal costs (estimated 2–3% of facility amount)
- Insurance requirements will increase monthly cost
- Processing time: 2–4 weeks from full documentation submission to approval
- Some banks may require farming/agricultural activity on the property; clarify if property is residential/hobby farm vs. commercial operation
